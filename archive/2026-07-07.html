The Rise of Electric Vehicles

Date: 2026-07-07 | Level: Beginner | Topic: Transportation and Environment

阅读这篇短文，了解电动汽车的普及和优势。

Electric vehicles, commonly known as EVs, are rapidly growing in popularity worldwide. Many governments are offering subsidies and tax breaks to encourage people to switch from gasoline cars to EVs. This shift is seen as a key step in fighting climate change and reducing air pollution in cities. Many countries have announced plans to phase out the sale of new gasoline cars within the next two decades.

电动汽车，通常称为EVs，正在全球迅速普及。许多政府提供补贴和税收减免，鼓励人们从汽油车转向电动汽车。这一转变被视为应对气候变化和减少城市空气污染的关键一步。许多国家已宣布计划在未来二十年内逐步停止销售新的汽油车。

One of the main benefits of EVs is their lower running cost. Electricity is generally cheaper than gasoline, and with fewer moving parts, EVs often need less maintenance. However, the initial purchase price can still be a barrier for many consumers, though prices are gradually falling as technology advances. But as battery production scales up, the cost is expected to drop further.

电动汽车的主要优势之一是运行成本较低。电通常比汽油便宜，而且由于活动部件较少，电动汽车通常需要更少的维护。然而，初始购买价格对许多消费者来说仍是一个障碍，但随着技术的进步，价格正在逐渐下降。但随着电池生产规模扩大，成本预计将进一步下降。

Battery range has improved significantly, and many new EVs can travel over 400 kilometers on a single charge. To address &#39;range anxiety,&#39; governments and companies are building more public charging stations, including fast chargers. As infrastructure improves, more people will likely choose EVs for their daily commute and long trips. Drivers can also charge their EVs at home overnight, which is very convenient.

电池续航里程有了显著提高，许多新款电动汽车单次充电可行驶超过400公里。为了解决“里程焦虑”，政府和公司正在建设更多的公共充电站，包括快速充电桩。随着基础设施的改善，更多人可能会选择电动汽车用于日常通勤和长途旅行。司机还可以在家中过夜充电，非常方便。
